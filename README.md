The classical Islamic concept of al-mu'allafatu qulubuhum ("those whose hearts are to be reconciled") has recently become a highly relevant and debated topic in Texas politics, intersecting directly with the 2026 U.S. Senate race and local real estate developments.

The application of this concept in Texas centers around a controversial fatwa (religious edict) issued in early 2026 by the Fiqh Council of North America (FCNA) and the Assembly of Muslim Jurists of America (AMJA). This fatwa argued that mandatory Zakat funds could be used to support political campaigns, politicians, and public policy efforts under the mu'allafatu qulubuhum category. The ruling cited the Prophet Muhammad's precedent of giving wealth to non-Muslim leaders to sway public interest and secure a general benefit for the Muslim community.
